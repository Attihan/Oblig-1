Flames engulfing Bjørvika

Library in Oslo put aflamed. The police has found the suspect.

Journalist: Judy Brown - 10/3/2023

Image source: Nico Franz/Randgruppe from Pixabay

Early in the morning, a young man was arrested after breaking into Bjorvika library the very same night. According to investigators, the suspect had made a bonfire in the middle of the third floor. OSLO, NORWAY - Bjørvika library suffered significant material damages in an unfortunate fire caused by a man who broke into the library last night.

The man made a camping fire right in the middle of the library, causing flames to spread throughout the premises. Police and the fire brigade were alerted about the incident around 1 a.m., and they rushed to the scene.

According to eyewitnesses, the man smashed a glass window to gain entry to the library. He then proceeded to build a camping fire in the middle of the library, ignoring the fire extinguishers and fire alarms in the vicinity.

"This is unheard of! How could anyone do this?” exclaimed Maria Davidsen, a librarian at Bjørvika library. “Thankfully, nobody was hurt, but the damages are extensive. The library is closed until necessary repairs have been made. The fire brigade struggled for two hours to put out the flames due to the books and furniture materials. The whole library suffered extensive smoke damage.

Police suspect the man, in his early 30s, was under the influence of drugs or alcohol. They arrested him at the scene and charged him with breaking and entering and arson. He is currently under investigation, and his motive is still unknown.

“It's a sad day for our community,” said Erik Johansen, a witness to the incident. “The library is a valuable resource for people in this area, and to see it suffer like this is heartbreaking.”

Aftermath

The repair cost for Bjørvika library is estimated to be substantial, with some reports suggesting it could reach up to 100 million NOK. This sad incident emphasizes the importance of taking care of our shared public spaces, and the need to ensure they remain a safe and accessible resource for everyone.
